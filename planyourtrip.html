PLAN YOUR TRIP

A sample of the temples that we can visit during your trip.

DAY 1: LUXOR

Luxor offers one of the most exquisite ancient tressures int he world. The Valley of Kings, the legend temple of Queen Hatshepsut,  Karnak Temple are one of the many things to explore the magic city of Luxor.

DAY 2: KARNAK - ESNA

Esna, a busy little farming town, located on the west bank of the Nile some 55 km south of Luxor. The Temple of Khnum, located 9 meter below streetlevel is one of the highlight in Esna. The lively tourist-oriented market in Esna can also bring some nice egyptian nile vibes.

DAY 3: CROSSING THE LOCK

The Esna lock links Esna to Aswan. In January 1996 Egypt opened the Lock. Both sides of the Lock have an elevation difference of 8 meters. Crossing the lock is always a very loud and joyfull experience and it is a scenic photo spot for the Nile travelers.

DAY 4: ESNA- EDFU

Watching the countryside with the little mud brick houses and observing the crops growing on the vertile land of the Nile, while sailing between Edfu and Esna, catapults you back in time. These paradisial views are ideal to relax, leave your worries behind and become one with nature.

DAY 5: SILSILA

During the 18th dynasty the Egyptians started using sandstone instead of limestones. Gebel el-Silsila became an important stone source for several famous ancient Egyptians temples. This spot is very serene because no parking is allowed for regular boats.

DAY 6: KOM OMBO

Dedicated to the god Sobek (the crocodile god) and Haroeris (the winged god of medicine and one of the oldest incarnations of Horus), Kom Ombo temple is known for its wall relieves showing ancient surgical and dental tools.
